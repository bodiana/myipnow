What Is a Ping Test?

A ping test sends packets to a host and measures round-trip time (RTT) in milliseconds. Use it to check if a server is reachable and how fast it responds.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between ping and traceroute?
Ping checks if a host is reachable and measures round-trip time. Traceroute maps every hop between you and the destination, showing where delays or failures occur along the route.
What is jitter and why does it matter?
Jitter is the variation in ping over time. Even with low average ping, high jitter causes stuttering in video calls and online games. Under 10ms jitter is ideal for real-time applications.
What does packet loss mean?
Packet loss occurs when data packets fail to reach their destination. Even 1-2% packet loss can cause problems in video calls and gaming. 0% is ideal.
Does a VPN affect ping?
Yes, a VPN typically increases ping because your traffic is routed through an additional server. The closer the VPN server to your destination, the smaller the impact on latency.
What is the difference between ping and latency?
Ping is the tool used to measure latency. Latency is the actual delay in milliseconds. They are often used interchangeably.
Can I ping an IP address?
Yes, you can ping both domain names and IP addresses. Enter any IPv4 address like 8.8.8.8 or a domain like google.com.

What Is Ping and How Does It Work?

Ping is one of the most fundamental network diagnostic tools. It works by sending ICMP (Internet Control Message Protocol) echo request packets to a target host and waiting for echo replies. The time between sending and receiving is measured in milliseconds and called round-trip time (RTT).

What does ping tell you?

Ping tells you three important things: whether a host is reachable, how fast it responds (latency), and how reliable the connection is (packet loss). A host that doesn't respond to ping may be offline, blocking ICMP traffic, or behind a firewall.

What is a good ping for gaming?

For online gaming, ping under 20ms is ideal. Under 50ms is good and most players won't notice any issues. Above 100ms you may experience lag, and above 150ms the game becomes noticeably delayed. Jitter — the variation in ping over time — is equally important for a smooth gaming experience.

What causes high ping?

The most common causes of high ping are physical distance to the server, network congestion, Wi-Fi interference, and ISP routing issues. Switching from Wi-Fi to a wired Ethernet connection typically reduces ping significantly. Using a VPN can also increase ping as traffic is routed through an additional server.

How to reduce ping?

To reduce ping: use a wired Ethernet connection instead of Wi-Fi, close background applications that use bandwidth, choose game or server locations closest to you, and contact your ISP if ping is consistently high. A quality router and modern network equipment also help.
