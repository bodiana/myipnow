DNS Lookup

Instant DNS Lookup to check A, AAAA, MX, CNAME, and TXT records for any domain. Find IP addresses and DNS details quickly.

What Is a DNS Lookup?

A DNS lookup is the process of querying the Domain Name System to translate a human-readable domain name like example.com into its corresponding IP address. Every time you visit a website, your device performs a DNS lookup automatically. This free DNS lookup tool lets you manually check DNS records for any domain to troubleshoot issues, verify configurations, or investigate a domain.

What DNS record types can I check?

The most common DNS record types are: A records (map a domain to an IPv4 address), AAAA records (map to IPv6), MX records (specify mail servers), NS records (identify authoritative name servers), TXT records (used for domain verification, SPF, and DKIM), and CNAME records (alias one domain to another).

How to use a DNS lookup to troubleshoot email

If your emails are not being delivered, check the MX records for your domain to verify they point to the correct mail server. Also check TXT records for SPF and DKIM entries which prevent emails from being marked as spam. Missing or incorrect records are a common cause of email delivery failures.

What is DNS propagation?

DNS propagation is the time it takes for DNS record changes to spread across all DNS servers worldwide. It can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours. If you recently changed your DNS records and they are not showing correctly, propagation may still be in progress.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a DNS lookup?
A DNS lookup queries the Domain Name System to translate a domain name into an IP address. It can also retrieve other DNS records like MX, NS, and TXT records that control how a domain handles email, traffic, and verification.
What is a DNS lookup and why is it important?
A DNS lookup resolves domain names to IP addresses and retrieves DNS records. It is important for troubleshooting website connectivity, verifying email configuration, and checking domain ownership.
How do I check DNS records for my domain?
Enter your domain name in the tool above and select the record type. You will see live DNS records including A, AAAA, MX, NS, and TXT records.
What do A, MX, NS, and TXT records mean?
A records map a domain to an IPv4 address. AAAA records map to IPv6. MX records specify mail servers. NS records identify name servers. TXT records are used for domain verification, SPF, and DKIM email authentication.
How long does DNS propagation take?
DNS propagation typically takes between a few minutes and 48 hours, depending on the TTL (Time to Live) settings of the DNS records and how quickly DNS servers around the world refresh their caches.
Why is my domain not showing DNS records?
Some domains have privacy protection or may not have certain record types configured. If you recently changed records, DNS propagation may still be in progress.
