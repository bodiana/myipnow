IPv4 vs IPv6

The internet primarily uses two types of IP addresses: IPv4 and IPv6. IPv4 addresses are composed of 32 bits, allowing for approximately 4.3 billion unique addresses. However, with the exponential growth of internet-connected devices, IPv4 addresses are running out. IPv6, on the other hand, uses 128 bits, creating an almost limitless number of unique addresses. Understanding the difference between these two can help you make informed decisions about your online presence.
